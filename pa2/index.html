Introduction

Retrovirus relies extensively on the expression of the sense virus genes. However, the retrovirus protein encoded from the antisense gene is getting attention because of its special biological significance. With the analysis of one species of retrovirus lentiviruses including HIV-1, we try to predict the antisense transcription genes and their encoded proteins.

To screen out potential antisense transcription genes , we used these criterions: the length of sequence , promoter site and poly-A site, phylogenetic analysis and protein secondary and tertiary structure prediction for the antisense sequences which is complementary sense gene. This research will help to deepen our understanding of the biological function and evolutionary relationship between antisense genes and antisense proteins.
